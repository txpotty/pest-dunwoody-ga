Comprehensive Squirrel Control Process

Our approach to squirrel removal in Dunwoody, GA is thorough and designed for long-term success. We start with a detailed inspection to understand the scope of the problem and identify the pathways squirrels are using to access your property. This isn't a one-size-fits-all service; we tailor our plan to the specific needs of your situation.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The first step involves our trained technician carefully examining your property, both inside and out. This includes the roof, attic, crawl spaces, vents, chimneys, and any other areas where squirrels might enter or nest. We look for signs like gnaw marks, droppings, tracks, and actual sightings of squirrels or their nests. Understanding the entry points is critical – it's not enough to just remove the current invaders; we need to stop new ones from coming in. During this assessment, we’ll discuss our findings with you and explain the proposed course of action.

Identifying Entry Points

Squirrels are surprisingly adept at finding small openings. They can chew through wood, enlarge existing gaps, or exploit damaged areas. Common entry points include gable vents, soffits, chimneys that aren't capped, torn screens, and even small cracks in the foundation or roofline. Our technicians are experts at spotting these often-overlooked access points.

Assessing the Extent of Infestation

Determining how many squirrels are present and where they are nesting helps us plan the most effective removal strategy. Sometimes, it's a single squirrel; other times, it could be a family. Their nesting location influences the trapping methods and the subsequent repair work needed.

Safe and Humane Removal

Once we understand the situation, we implement our humane removal plan. We utilize various techniques and tools designed to capture squirrels without causing them harm. Our goal is to safely relocate the squirrels away from your property.

Humane Trapping Methods

Live traps are often the preferred method for squirrel removal. These traps capture the animal without injury. We carefully place traps in areas where we've observed squirrel activity or near their suspected entry points. Regular monitoring of these traps is essential to ensure the well-being of the captured animal.

Relocation Protocols

After a squirrel is caught in a live trap, we follow local regulations and best practices for relocation. This typically involves transporting the squirrel a safe distance away from your property to a suitable natural habitat where they can thrive. This process is carried out as quickly and stress-free as possible for the animal.

Exclusion and Prevention

Removing the current squirrels is only half the battle. To prevent future infestations, we focus on sealing the entry points they used and implementing preventive measures.

Sealing Entry Points

This is a critical step in our process. Using durable materials that squirrels cannot easily gnaw through, we seal all identified entry points. This might involve repairing damaged areas, installing chimney caps, screening vents, or reinforcing vulnerable spots. Proper exclusion is key to ensuring your home remains squirrel-free in the long term.

Preventative Measures

Beyond sealing entry points, we can offer advice and implement additional measures to make your property less attractive to squirrels. Signs You Might Need Squirrel Removal in Dunwoody

Squirrels, while a common part of the Dunwoody landscape, become a problem when they decide your home or business is a suitable place to nest. Recognizing the signs of a squirrel infestation early is important for minimizing damage and getting help from professionals for effective squirrel removal in Dunwoody, GA. Here are some common indicators that you may have unwanted squirrel visitors:

Noises in Attic or Walls

One of the most obvious signs of squirrels in your home is the sounds they make. Squirrels are most active during the day, especially in the morning and late afternoon. If you hear scratching, scurrying, or gnawing noises coming from your attic, walls, or ceiling during these times, it's a strong indication that squirrels are present. Unlike rodents which may be active at night, squirrels are diurnal, meaning they are awake and moving around during daylight hours. These sounds can be particularly concerning if they are persistent and seem to be centered in a specific area.

The sounds you hear can vary depending on what the squirrels are doing. They might be moving around, building a nest, or gnawing on materials. The location of the noises can also give clues about where they are nesting. Sounds directly above you might indicate an attic infestation, while noises in the walls suggest they have found a pathway within the structural elements of your home. These sounds aren't just annoying; they are a clear signal that action is needed to prevent damage to your property. Residential properties are particularly susceptible to this kind of issue.

Visible Damage to Property

Squirrels have a strong instinct to gnaw, and they will chew on a variety of materials to keep their teeth sharp and to gain access to areas. This gnawing can cause significant damage to your property. Look for chewed wood, rubber, plastic, and even electrical wires. Evidence of gnawing around entry points like vents, soffits, and rooflines is a clear sign of squirrel activity. They might also damage insulation in attics, making it less effective and potentially contaminating it with droppings and urine.

Beyond structural elements, squirrels can damage other parts of your property. They might chew on outdoor furniture, bird feeders, or garden plants. Inside, they can damage stored items in attics or garages. The extent of the damage can range from minor cosmetic issues to major structural problems, depending on how long the infestation has been present and where the squirrels have been active. Addressing this damage requires professional attention, both for removal and potentially for repair.

Presence of Droppings or Nests

Finding squirrel droppings or nests on your property is another strong indicator of an infestation. Squirrel droppings are typically oblong and about the size of a raisin. They are often found near entry points, in attics, or in other areas where squirrels are active. The presence of droppings not only confirms an infestation but also poses a hygiene risk.

Squirrels build nests, often called dreys, in safe and sheltered locations. In homes, attics are a common nesting site. These nests are typically made from leaves, twigs, insulation, and other soft materials. Finding a nest confirms that squirrels are not just passing through but have established a presence on your property. Locating and removing these nests is an important part of the removal process. Sometimes, our rodent control services are needed if other unwelcome guests have made their way in alongside the squirrels.

Chewed Wires or Electrical Issues

Perhaps one of the most concerning problems associated with squirrel infestations is their tendency to chew on electrical wires. Squirrels may gnaw on wires in attics, walls, or around the exterior of your home. This can create a serious fire hazard. If you notice unexplained power outages, flickering lights, or the smell of burning insulation, squirrels chewing on wires could be the cause.

Damage to electrical wiring requires immediate attention from a qualified electrician and professional squirrel removal to prevent further damage. It's a hidden danger that can have devastating consequences. Never assume that just because you don't see the squirrels, they aren't causing problems. The damage they inflict on electrical systems can remain unseen until a dangerous situation arises.

Seeing Squirrels Entering or Exiting Property

The most definitive sign of a squirrel problem is actually seeing squirrels repeatedly entering or exiting your home or business through a specific point. Observe your property, especially in the mornings and evenings, to see if you can spot squirrels using a particular opening in your roofline, siding, or around vents. This visual confirmation helps pinpoint their access routes.

Observing squirrels carrying nesting materials, such as leaves or twigs, into your home further confirms that they are setting up a residence. Seeing them regularly going in and out indicates an established pattern of behavior that requires professional intervention. Don't hesitate to contact us if you've witnessed this behavior; it provides valuable information for our removal strategy. This is a common issue we see in both residential and commercial properties.

The Risks of Ignoring a Squirrel Infestation

Ignoring a squirrel problem won't make it go away; in fact, it will likely get worse and lead to more significant issues. Squirrels are prolific breeders and can establish large populations quickly. The longer they remain on your property, the more damage they can cause and the harder it becomes to remove them.

Structural Damage

Squirrels are constantly gnawing, and this can lead to significant structural damage over time. They will chew on wood beams, support structures, and even plumbing pipes. This damage can weaken the integrity of your home and require costly repairs. They can also damage siding, trim, and other exterior elements as they chew their way in. The cost of repairing this kind of structural damage can far exceed the cost of professional squirrel removal.

Insulation Damage and Contamination

Attics are a favorite nesting spot for squirrels, and they will tear up and displace insulation to create comfortable nests. This not only reduces the effectiveness of your home's insulation, leading to higher energy bills, but also contaminates it with droppings and urine. Squirrel waste can harbor bacteria and unpleasant odors, creating an unhealthy environment in your home. Damaged and contaminated insulation often needs to be completely removed and replaced.

Fire Hazards from Chewed Wires

As mentioned earlier, squirrels chewing on electrical wires is a serious fire hazard. Damaged wires can cause short circuits, sparks, and ultimately, fires. This is one of the most dangerous consequences of a squirrel infestation and requires immediate action to prevent a catastrophic event. Never underestimate the risk posed by squirrels damaging electrical systems in your home or business.

Spread of Parasites and Diseases

Squirrels can carry parasites like fleas, ticks, and mites, which can then infest your home and potentially bite your family and pets. They can also carry diseases, though the risk of transmission to humans is generally low, it's still a possibility. Dealing with a squirrel infestation can sometimes necessitate flea treatment or tick control services after removal. It's better to be safe than sorry when it comes to potential health risks associated with wildlife in your home.

Unpleasant Odors

Squirrel urine and feces can create strong, unpleasant odors that permeate your home. These odors can be difficult to eliminate and can make your living space uncomfortable. The lingering smell can also attract other pests. Proper cleanup and sanitization are necessary after squirrel removal to address these odor issues.
